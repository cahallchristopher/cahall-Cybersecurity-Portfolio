### Explanation of Changes

1. **Aligning with SOC Workflows:** The revised bullets now explicitly use SOC workflows such as "triage" and "incident response," making it clear how the experience aligns with the role's responsibilities.

2. **Emphasizing Lab-Based Experience:** By specifying lab-based simulations, we ensure that the work is clearly defensible and avoids any implication of production experience.

3. **Adding Specific Tools and Techniques:** Using terms like "Splunk" for SIEM, "Wireshark," and specific network protocols (HTTP, DNS, TCP) highlights the technical depth of the experience.

4. **Focus on Observations and Outcomes:** The revised bullets focus more on what was observed and concluded from the analysis rather than just the tools used.

5. **Clear Communication and Documentation:** Emphasizing the documentation aspect ensures that communication skills are highlighted in a way that aligns with the job description's requirement for clear reporting.

6. **Vulnerability Management:** Highlighting vulnerability assessment efforts shows preparedness to handle security threats, which is critical for an entry-level SOC role.

7. **Evidence Collection and Analysis:** The inclusion of evidence collection and analysis highlights the ability to gather relevant data for incident response, a key component in the new job description.

By making these changes, the resume now more accurately reflects the skills and experience needed for an entry-level SOC Analyst position while maintaining defensibility and relevance to the role.
